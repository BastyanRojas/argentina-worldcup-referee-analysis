# Multi-Margin Surface, Leverage Gradient & Field-Wide Call Quality
_Generated by `src/multimargin_leverage.py` from `data/box_exposure_teammatch.csv` (StatsBomb events, WC2022) and `data/var_decisions_2022.csv` (ESPN VAR review, field-wide)._

Answers the two objections left open by `BIAS_IDENTIFICATION.md`: does the asymmetry generalize across referee-discretion margins (M), does it track decision leverage (L), and do dubious calls favor everyone equally (E)?
## Design M — the five-margin decision surface (WC2022)
Signed so **positive residual = treatment consistent with favoritism** (for cards received, fewer-than-expected is positive). Each margin has its own opportunity exposure. Pre-registered direction: favoritism predicts positive residuals.

| Margin | Leverage (goal-eq) | Arg obs | Arg expected | Signed residual (SD) | p (favored tail) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Penalty awarded | 0.783 | 5 | 1.6 | +2.64 | 0.025 |
| Dangerous FK won | 0.027 | 5 | 5.2 | -0.08 | 0.592 |
| Foul won (any) | 0.008 | 132 | 114.0 | +1.68 | 0.054 |
| Yellow received (flip) | 0.030 | 16 | 14.3 | -0.44 | 0.727 |
| Opponent yellow drawn | 0.030 | 23 | 17.1 | +1.43 | 0.099 |

- **The surface is one-margin.** Argentina is extreme only on penalties (+2.64 SD). Fouls won, dangerous free kicks and opponents' cards are all within noise of expectation; on **yellows received they are on the *punished* side** (-0.44 SD signed = more carded per foul than the field). 'Every margin favors them' is false in this data.
- Leverage values: penalty and dangerous-FK leverage are calibrated from the event data (mean StatsBomb penalty xG; direct-FK xG per dangerous foul won); the foul and card leverages are order-of-magnitude assumptions (0.008 / 0.03 goal-equivalents). The gradient test below is ordinal, so only the *ranking* matters — and penalties being an order of magnitude above every other margin is not in doubt.
## Design L — the leverage gradient
If favoritism were instrumental, treatment should concentrate where decisions are worth the most. Per team: slope of signed residual on log10(leverage) across the five margins.

- **Argentina's gradient: +0.88 SD per leverage decade — rank 3/32** (field median -0.05). Spearman of residual vs leverage, Argentina: rho = 0.21 (p = 0.74, n = 5 margins).
- Read honestly: with five margins this is structured description, not sharp inference. What it shows is a **steeply positive treatment-vs-leverage profile, the near-steepest of the field** — favored specifically where a decision is worth the most (the penalty spot) and *not* on the cheap margins. Consistent with 'bias where it matters'; equally consistent with a penalty-specific anomaly of any origin. It rules out generalized, all-margin favoritism.
## Design E — field-wide VAR call quality (the full-field Design B)
ESPN's review of every VAR decision of WC2022 yields **45 graded decisions**, of which **12 are dubious** (soft / debatable / wrong / harsh / missed intervention). Under neutral refereeing, dubious interventions should scatter across matches uniformly and favor either side of a match with probability 1/2.

| Beneficiary of dubious VAR decisions | Count |
|---|---|
| **Argentina** | **4** |
| Portugal | 2 |
| France | 1 |
| Ghana | 1 |
| Tunisia | 1 |
| Saudi Arabia | 1 |
| Netherlands | 1 |
| Iran | 1 |

- **Argentina: 4 of the 12 dubious decisions in the tournament (33%), the most of any team** — including the only 'missed red card' flagged in the final and a penalty graded an outright wrong intervention (Poland).

**Under the match-uniform null** (dubious interventions scatter uniformly over the 64 matches, coin-flip beneficiary):

- Allocation: 4 of 12 dubious decisions in Argentina matches (7 of 64), p = 0.034. Direction: 4/4 favored Argentina, p = 0.062. Joint: **p = 0.0032**; selection-corrected (any of 32 teams ≥ 4): **p = 0.021**.

**Under the stricter decision-conditioned null** (condition on where the 45 graded decisions actually occurred — 9 were in Argentina matches, reflecting the final and extra-time knockouts — and on whom each favored; permute only which 12 are dubious):

- Allocation: p = 0.21. Argentina ≥ 4 dubious-favorable: p = 0.035 (hypergeometric; permutation check 0.033). Selection-corrected: **p = 0.05**.
- Neither null strictly dominates: the graded-decision set is itself endogenous (more dubious officiating attracts more reviews), so conditioning on it absorbs part of the signal under test, while the match-uniform null ignores that finals and extra-time games mechanically draw more reviews. The honest statement is the range: **selection-corrected p between 0.021 and 0.05** depending on the null.
- This answers the objection 'every team gets dubious calls': they do — but no other team got more than 2, and none had every dubious call in their matches break their own way.
- **Caveats owned:** the gradings are one outlet's judgments (ESPN), extracted from prose; the binarization to 'dubious' (soft/debatable/wrong/harsh/missed) was done by this project **non-blind**, knowing the hypothesis; and the dataset covers VAR-reviewed decisions only, not on-field calls that VAR silently confirmed (the Netherlands QF and Croatia SF penalties are therefore absent — a selection that biases these tests *against* Argentina effects). Counts this small demand the exact tests used here.
## What this adds to the verdict
- **Sharpens the claim:** the asymmetry is penalty-specific (M), sits at the top of the leverage gradient (L), and its dubious-call signature leads the field (E: selection-corrected p = 0.021 under the match-uniform null, 0.05 under the stricter decision-conditioned null — significant under one defensible null, marginal under the other).
- **Bounds the claim:** no generalized favoritism — Argentina pays full price on cards and gains nothing on cheap margins. Any mechanism consistent with this data must operate narrowly, at the highest-leverage decision, in both 2022 and (provisionally) 2026.
